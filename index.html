I am a first-year PhD student (2024 Fall-) in Computer Science at the University of Southern California, supervised by Prof. Mohammad Soleymani and Dr. Wei-Wen(Andrew) Feng.

I received my Master’s degree from University of Southern California. During my Master’s, I took Research Internships at Motorola Solutions and at TuSimple. Previously, I was a Research Engineer at YITU Technology for one year. Prior to that, I got my Bachelor degree from Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

My research interests mainly lie in Video Perception and Generative Models.

selected publications

  1. Lossless Face Anonymization on Downstream Tasks
    Zhangyu Jin, Siddharth Agrawal, and Yanyan Hu
    Nov 2023
    Abs HTML

    Completed Face Anonymization by substituting each face with a similar one from the fakely generated face gallery, effectively hiding facial information from all users. Utilized as both the training and testing data to guarantee no decline in performance for all downstream tasks (ReID, Object Detection).

  2. MemoryFlow: Efficient Video Object Detection
    Zhangyu Jin, Haiping Wu, and Sha Long
    Aug 2022
    Abs HTML

    Innovated MemoryFlow for efficient Video Object Detection, integrating neighboring optical flow content and long-term sequential content from memory. Reached 2nd place in ImageNet-VID ranking under ResNet-50 with fewer reference information and a faster inference time.

  3. Real-time Panoptic Segmentation for Autonomous Driving
    Zhangyu Jin, Yikai Hua, and Wenbin Lu
    Jul 2021
    Abs HTML

    Incubated the Panoptic Segmentation for Autonomous Vehicles from scratch, providing prior information for all downstream tasks. Accomplished training-based Model Compression to reduce inference time, resulting in YITUTech’s successful completion of the first road test.
